Manglaralto and the coastal corridor

The Manglaralto–Montañita–Olón corridor offers a relaxed, surf-oriented coastal lifestyle with strong community ties, easy access to nature, and a growing expat presence. Here's what life looks like.

Manglaralto (where the property is)

A Place Where Time Stands Still

Manglaralto is a smaller, family-oriented fishing village within the larger parroquia (district) of the same name. Working-class coastal families, fishermen, and an increasing number of expats and remote workers.

Strong sense of community and environmental awareness. Residents are active in local turtle conservation and festivals.

Practical Living

Groceries & Shopping

Local markets + new Tuti grocery in town. TIA supermarket in Montañita ($1.50 by bus). Larger stores in Santa Elena/Salinas.

Quality Meat & Provisions

Specialized shops like Piedra Carnisariato provide fresh, quality meats and local products throughout Manglaralto.

Healthcare Local health center for basic care. Private clinics for labs, X-rays. Serious care: La Libertad/Salinas or Guayaquil.
Internet Fiber optic available. Surprisingly reliable. UPS backup recommended for occasional outages.
Getting Around Many expats skip car ownership. Taxi to Olón: ~$2. Bus to Montañita: $0.60. Two taxi stations in town, WhatsApp to request pickup.
Safety Relatively safe compared to larger Ecuadorian cities. Standard precautions: no valuables on beach at night. Not the dangerous coast reputation (that's Manta, Esmeraldas).

Events & Culture

Annual Carnival with parades, music, dancing. New Year's Eve is something to experience. Ocaso Bar has Friday night live music — local blues and rock bands doing American covers or original work.

Local Music & Nightlife

Ocaso Bar is a community favorite for Friday night live music with local blues and rock bands. A great place to meet neighbors and experience the local music scene.

Community Gathering Spaces

The town square and parks are where community life happens. Festivals, celebrations, and everyday social gatherings make Manglaralto feel like home.

Faith & Tradition

The local iglesia (church) sits at the heart of Manglaralto's cultural identity. Residents value tradition and community bonds forged through generations.

Local Cafés & Gathering Spots

Sweet Place and other local bakery cafés are where expats and locals connect over morning coffee and fresh pastries. These casual meeting spots become part of daily life.

Practical Necessities

Quality hardware stores like Omnisariato make home maintenance and projects straightforward. Everything you need for living is within reach.

Wellness & Fitness

Local gyms and fitness options keep you active. Whether it's surfing, beach walks, or gym workouts, staying fit is easy here.

Fresh Seafood Dining

Esquina de Doma and other local restaurants serve fresh-caught seafood. Eating where fishermen sell their daily catch means incredible flavor and value.

Artisanal Bakery Culture

Bakeries like Ruta Pan y Canela serve fresh-baked bread and pastries daily. Part of the charm of Manglaralto is these small, quality establishments.

Diverse Dining Options

Restaurants like Laurel offer quality meals in a relaxed setting. The dining scene reflects both local tradition and growing international influences.

Growing Tourism & Development

New accommodations like Hotel Cuervo Blanco signal Manglaralto's growing appeal while maintaining its authentic character.

Quality Dining & Entertainment

High-end wine and spirits shops cater to expats and visitors seeking finer things. The culinary scene is evolving to serve diverse tastes.

Modern Convenience

Tuti represents the new generation of grocery shopping in Manglaralto—modern, convenient, and community-focused.

Dos Mangas (~15 min inland)

Waterfall Hikes & Nature

Local pickup trucks from Manglaralto: ~$0.50/person (if you wait for 6) or $3 for immediate departure.

Trail entrance at interpretation center. Self-guided: $2. Guide: ~$22.50. Boots rentable for $0.50 if muddy. Open ~8:00–15:00.

2-hour easy walk through humid forest to waterfalls. Cool, clear water for swimming. Howler monkeys above the canopy, medicinal plants, endangered Guayacán trees.

Horseback riding: ~$10/person for guided forest trail rides. Operators in Dos Mangas.

Birdwatching: Toucans, tanagers, warblers, howler monkeys.

Olón (~8 min north)

Quiet Beach Town, Great Food

Long stretch of sand, casual beachfront cabins, mix of surfers, families, and expats. Quieter than Montañita. Sundays popular with day visitors from Guayaquil.

Restaurants: MOMO (cocktails, international), South Indian Restaurant (excellent chicken tikka), La Churrería (churros), Noah Cafe (sandwiches, coffee), Almacigo, Olón Espresso, Roadhouse, Il Pirata...

Community: Strong expat/retiree presence. Weekly gatherings at Jerry's Cabana (Saturdays), poker nights, aerial dance performances at community center.

Surfing: Gentler waves than Montañita — good for learning. Several surf schools with English instruction.

Montañita (~5 min north)

Surf Town + Nightlife

Famous surf-centric party town. Powerful right-breaking waves (intermediate to advanced). Dense cluster of surf schools and rental shops.

Nightlife: Lost Beach Club (world-famous electronic music, Monday nights), Cocktail Alley, Nativa Bambú, Montañita Brewery, Tiki Limbo...

Food: Ezzios Pizza (delivers), El Pigro (Italian), La Cadena (breakfast smoothies, pancakes, cocktails).

Community & Culture

Montañita's welcoming culture and crafts shops reflect its bohemian spirit. A place where locals, expats, and travelers come together.

Bohemian, walkable streets. Handicraft shops, hostels, bars. Loud late into the night but intimate by global party-town standards.

Activities Within 30 Minutes

Surfing Montañita (advanced), Olón (beginners). Multiple schools.
Whale Watching June–October, peak July–September. Day trips to Puerto López or watch from the property.
Waterfall Hikes Dos Mangas, 2-hour easy trail, swimming at the falls.
Horseback Riding Dos Mangas, ~$10/person for forest trails.
Birdwatching Dos Mangas forest — toucans, tanagers, howler monkeys.
Fishing Informal shore fishing. Some local boats accept passengers.

Why This Corridor?

vs. Salinas

Salinas is a larger city beach with more urban infrastructure. This corridor is more relaxed, surf-oriented, community-driven. Easier access to nature, fewer crowds, stronger expat community.

vs. Puerto López

Puerto López is the premier whale-watching destination. This corridor compensates with better surf, more nightlife options, and a stronger digital-nomad scene. Whale watching still accessible via day trips.

What Makes It Unique

Fishing-village authenticity (Manglaralto) + quiet surf lifestyle (Olón) + world-class surf and nightlife (Montañita) — all within a compact, walkable coastal strip. Live in the tranquil village, access excitement in 10 minutes.
